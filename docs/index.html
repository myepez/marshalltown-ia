Property Tax Rates

Background

I created these 3D visualizations to help the public and city representatives view Marshalltown's tax rates in a more accessible format than traditional spreadsheets. My goal is to help decision-makers identify which areas have developed into valuable properties that contribute to Marshalltown's wealth, and which areas need support to become stronger contributors to our city.

Data

The City of Marshalltown and Marshall County staff generously provided CSV files containing approximately 11,000 records from the year 2023 with parcel IDs, acres, addresses, and property tax totals. The raw data required cleaning before processing. About 500 records were removed where the data was ambiguous and would have required speculation to interpret. I used Google Spreadsheets and Python scripts to clean the bulk of the data, while manually verifying about 500 records using Google Maps and the Beacon web application. All data points can be independently verified by checking parcel IDs or addresses through the publicly accessible Beacon application and the interactive map.

Maps

The maps below use coordinates found using the Python library Nominatim and the addresses provided. Each coordinate height is determined by the tax rate the coordinate pays. The tax rate is calculated by the ratio of the total paid in 2023 and the size of the land in acres. Coordinates that are red mean that they participate at a $22,600/acre tax rate that is sustainable for a city $120 M/year budget. Coordinates turn yellow the further they are from the rate.
